Help to find and apply for the right benefits for you

Benefit checkers and calculators

To find out which benefits you might be eligible for and how much you might get, you can:

  • check what regular payments you could get if you're looking for work
  • use the Pension Credit calculator
  • use a benefit calculator from an independent organisation
If you're already getting tax credits or Housing Benefit, these will stop if you or your partner apply for Universal Credit.

Advice

These organisations can help you to find information and advice about work, redundancy, benefits and debt.

  • Advice Local
  • Citizen's Advice
  • Money Advice Service

BSL and alternative formats

If you use British Sign Language (BSL), you can use the video relay service. If you need alternative formats, such as braille, large print or audio CD, call the relevant service.

Find the video relay link and telephone number for:

  • Universal Credit
  • New Style Job Seeker's Allowance
  • Pension Credit

Support visits if you need help to claim benefits

You may be able to get a home visit from a Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) officer if you need extra help to claim benefits, for example because you:

  • have complex needs
  • are disabled
  • are a vulnerable young person making a claim for the first time
  • have nobody else to support you
  • cannot claim benefits in any other way
