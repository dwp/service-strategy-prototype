Further help if you’re disabled, caring for someone, or looking after a family

You might be able to get further financial help depending on your needs and circumstances.

If you're on a low income

You could get help with housing costs, Council Tax, public transport costs, NHS costs, one off expenses, home adaptations and heating your home.

Financial help if you're on a low income

If you're temporarily unable to work

You could get help if you cannot work because you're ill or self-isolating. This includes Statutory Sick Pay, Universal Credit, and New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA).

Financial help if you're temporarily unable to work

If you're disabled or have a health condition

You could get additional support if you're disabled or have a health condition. This includes both financial help and support with finding and staying in work.

Support if you're disabled or have a health condition

If you're caring for someone

If you regularly spend time caring for someone, you could get Carer's Allowance, Carer's Credit or an extra amount in your Universal Credit or Pension Credit payment.

Financial help if you're caring for someone

If you're looking after a family

You could get further support if you look after children, plan to have a baby, or are adopting a child. This includes Child Benefit, financial support if you take time off work, help with childcare costs, support if you're studying, and extra financial help if your child is disabled.

Financial help for families

If someone dies

If someone has died, you might be able to get help with funeral costs. You might also be able to get regular payments such as Bereavement Support Payment or Guardian's Allowance.

Financial help if someone has died
