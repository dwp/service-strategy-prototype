Based on your answers, you might not be able to get these benefits

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

New Style JSA - why you might not be eligible

You can only get New Style JSA if:

  • you're under the State Pension age
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions for 16 and 17 year olds)
  • you’re available for work
  • you’re not in full-time education
  • you’re not working, or are working less than 16 hours per week on average
  • you do not have an illness or disability which stops you from working
  • you have paid or been credited with enough National Insurance contributions, usually over the last 2 to 3 years

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland

Universal Credit

Universal Credit - why you might not be eligible

You can only get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re under State Pension age, or your partner is
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

If you want to apply anyway, find out how to apply for Universal Credit.

Pension Credit

Pension Credit - why you might not be eligible

You can only get Pension Credit if you and your partner have both reached State Pension age, or one of you is getting Housing Benefit for people over State Pension age. You can start an application for Pension Credit up to 4 months before you reach State Pension age.

If you want to apply for Pension Credit anyway, find out how:

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland

Further help

Further help might be available if:

  • you're on a low income
  • you're temporarily unable to work
  • you're disabled or have a health condition
  • you're caring for someone
  • you're looking after a family
  • someone has died
