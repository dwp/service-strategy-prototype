Based on your answers, this is what you could get

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

New Style JSA - why you might be eligible

To get New Style JSA, you must be under State Pension age and working less than 16 hours per week on average.

You also need to have paid or been credited with enough National Insurance contributions, usually over the last 2 to 3 years.

Your savings and your partner’s income will not affect your claim.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style JSA. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit - why you might be eligible

To get Universal Credit, you or your partner must be under State Pension age, and you must have £16,000 or less in savings between you.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style Jobseeker's Allowance. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

You have to apply as a couple if you and your partner live together.

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit, even if the claim is not approved.

You might not be able to get Pension Credit.

Pension Credit - why you might not be eligible

You can only get Pension Credit if you and your partner have both reached State Pension age, or one of you is getting Housing Benefit for people over State Pension age. You can start an application for Pension Credit up to 4 months before you reach State Pension age.
