Regular payments if you're looking for work

Depending on your situation, you might be able to get regular payments if you're looking for work.

If you're under State Pension age

If you're under State Pension age, you might be able to get Universal Credit or New Style Jobseeker's Allowance, or both. You will be expected to prepare for and look for work while you're getting this support.

New Style Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)

You could get New Style JSA if you're under State Pension age, are unemployed or normally work less than 16 hours per week. You also need to have made enough National Insurance contributions, usually over the last 2 to 3 years. Your savings and partner’s income will not affect your claim. You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style JSA. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Find out more or apply for New Style JSA

Universal Credit

You could get Universal Credit if you have £16,000 or less in savings and you or your partner is under State Pension age. If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit. You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style JSA. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Find out more or apply for Universal Credit

If you're over State Pension age

If you're over State Pension age, you can get financial support without being expected to look for work.

You might be able to get Pension Credit if you and your partner have both reached State Pension age. You could still get it if one of you is under State Pension age and one of you is getting Housing Benefit for people over State Pension age.

Find out more or apply for Pension Credit

If you have a limited ability to work

If you have a health condition or disability that limits your ability to work, you might be able to get further financial support.
