Being unable to work

You might be eligible for Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

Tell your employer that you have symptoms of coronavirus (COVID-19). You might be able to get SSP from day one of your illness.

After the first 7 days of absence, your employer may ask you for an isolation note to show you cannot work because of coronavirus (COVID-19). You can get an isolation note from NHS 111 online.

The law is currently changing so that if you’ve been ill from coronavirus (COVID-19) since 13 March 2020 and you’re eligible for SSP, you will get a backdated payment.

If you’re not eligible for SSP

If you cannot get SSP, you can check what other financial support to apply for.
