Being unable to work

Tell your employer that someone you live with has symptoms of coronavirus (COVID-19)

You must stay at home if someone you live with has symptoms of coronavirus (COVID-19). This is sometimes known as ‘self-isolating’.

If you can work from home

You should work from home if you can. Speak to your employer about working from home.

If you cannot work from home

Your employer should tell you if you’re covered by their sick leave or special leave policy.

Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

If you cannot get sick pay or special leave from your employer, you might be able to get SSP from day one of your self-isolation.

The law is currently changing so that if you’ve been ill from coronavirus (COVID-19) since 13 March 2020 and you’re eligible for SSP, you will get a backdated payment.

If you cannot get sick pay, special leave or SSP

If you're not eligible for sick pay, special leave or SSP, you can check what other financial support to apply for.
