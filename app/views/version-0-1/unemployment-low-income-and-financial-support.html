Check what support you could apply for

You might be able to get financial support (‘benefits’) if you:

  • have been made unemployed
  • are on a low income
  • have been told not to work by your employer (you're 'on furlough')
  • are self-employed and earning less

Benefits you might get

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a monthly payment to help with your living costs if you’re on a low income, out of work or you cannot work.

You might be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (check if you can get Universal Credit if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

Check how to apply for Universal Credit.

If you’re working

You might still get Universal Credit if you're working. Your Universal Credit payments will be reduced as you earn more and go up if you earn less.

New Style Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)

New Style JSA is a payment every 2 weeks to help you while you look for work. To get it, you’ll need to have worked as an employee and paid enough National Insurance contributions, usually in the last 2 to 3 years.

You'll also need to:

  • be 18 or over
  • be under State Pension age
  • not be in full-time education
  • not be working at the moment, or be working less than 16 hours a week
  • live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • have the right to work in the UK
If you're eligible for Universal Credit, you might be able to get it with or instead of New Style JSA.

Check how to apply for New Style JSA.

Other support if you’re getting less or no work

You might be able to get other financial support if you are unemployed or on a low income. This includes:

  • Employment and Support Allowance, if you have a health condition or disability that affects how much you can work
  • support if you have a disability
  • help with your Council Tax
  • Pension Credit to help with cost of living in retirement
  • help with mortgage interest payments, if you get Universal Credit or Pension Credit
