Regular payments if you're temporarily unable to work

The following payments are available, depending on your situation. For example, if you're ill, on furlough, shielding or self-isolating.

Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

If you’re currently employed, you might be able to get a weekly payment if you're too ill to work.

Eligibility

You might qualify for SSP if you:

  • are classed as an employee and have done some work for your employer
  • earn an average of at least £120 per week
  • have been ill, self-isolating or shielding for at least 4 days in a row (including non-working days)

How to apply for SSP

New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

New Style ESA gives you money to help with living costs if you’re unable to work, and support to get back into work if you’re able to. You can apply for New Style ESA if you’re employed, self-employed or unemployed.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style ESA. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Eligibility

You might be able to get New Style ESA if you:

  • are under the State Pension age
  • are disabled or have a health condition that affects how much you can work
  • or your child might have coronavirus (COVID-19) or you’re recovering from it
  • or your child are self-isolating because you came into contact with someone who might have coronavirus
  • have been told to stay at home for at least 12 weeks by the NHS because you’re at high risk of severe illness
  • have worked as an employee or been self-employed and have paid enough National Insurance contributions in the last 2 to 3 years – National Insurance Credits also count
  • do not get Statutory Sick Pay (although you can apply 3 months before it’s due to end), Jobseeker’s Allowance or the severe disability premium

How to apply for New Style ESA

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

New Style JSA is a fortnightly payment if you’re available and looking for work.

To get it, you must be under State Pension age and be unemployed or working less than 16 hours a week.

You also need to have paid or been credited with enough National Insurance contributions over the last 2 to 3 years.

Your savings and your partner’s income will not affect how much New Style JSA you’re paid.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style JSA. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Find out more about New Style JSA:

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland
You cannot get New Style JSA if you get or are entitled to severe disability premium. You might be able to get contribution based or income-based JSA.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a monthly payment if you’re on a low income or out of work.

To get it, you or your partner must be under State Pension age, and you must have £16,000 or less in savings between you.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style Jobseeker's Allowance. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Find out more about Universal Credit

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Other help is also available if:

  • you're looking for work
  • you're on a low income
  • you're disabled or have a health condition
  • you're caring for someone
  • you're looking after a family
  • someone has died
