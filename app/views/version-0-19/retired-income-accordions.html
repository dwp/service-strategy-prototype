Regular payments if you've reached State Pension age

The following payments are available, depending on your situation.

State Pension

If you've reached State Pension age, you might be able to get your State Pension. You can still get it even if you decide to carry on working.

How to apply for your State Pension

Pension Credit

Pension Credit tops up your weekly income if it’s below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples).

To get it, you must have reached State Pension age. If you have a partner, they must also have reached State Pension age, unless you’re getting Housing Benefit.

You might still be able to get Pension Credit even if you have savings, have a pension or own your home.

Find out more about Pension Credit:

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a monthly payment if you’re on a low income or out of work.

To get it, you or your partner must be under State Pension age, and you must have £16,000 or less in savings between you.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style Jobseeker's Allowance. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

How to apply for Universal Credit

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.
