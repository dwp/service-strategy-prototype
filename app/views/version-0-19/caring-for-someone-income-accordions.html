Regular payments if you're caring for someone

The following financial support is available, depending on your situation.

Carer's Allowance

Carer's Allowance is a weekly payment to help cover the cost of caring for someone.

You could get it if:

  • the person you're caring for gets certain benefits
  • you’re 16 or over
  • you spend at least 35 hours a week caring for someone
  • you normally live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • you’re not studying for 21 hours a week or more
  • you earn £128 or less a week after tax, National Insurance and expenses

You do not have to be related to, or live with, the person you care for.

You do not get paid extra if you care for more than one person.

If someone else also cares for the same person as you, only one of you can claim Carer’s Allowance.

How to apply for Carer's Allowance

Carer's Credit

Carer's Credit helps to fill gaps in your National Insurance record. This means you can care for someone without affecting your ability to qualify for the State Pension.

You could get it if:

  • the person you're caring for gets certain benefits
  • you’re 16 or over
  • under State Pension age
  • you spend at least 20 hours a week caring for someone

Regular payments for the person you care for

Attendance Allowance

The person you are caring for might be eligible for Attendance Allowance.

Attendance Allowance helps with extra costs if someone has a disability severe enough that they need someone to help look after them.

They might be able to get it if:

  • they've reached State Pension age
  • they're disabled

How to apply for Attendance Allowance

The person you are caring for might be able to get other financial support if they are disabled or have a health condition.

Other help is also available if:

  • you're looking for work
  • you're temporarily unable to work
  • you're on a low income
  • you're disabled or have a health condition
  • you're looking after a family
  • someone has died
