You might be able to get financial support

Based on your answers, you might be able to get Universal Credit and New Style Jobseeker's Allowance.

Should I claim one or both of these benefits?

If you're eligible for both Universal Credit and New Style Jobseeker's Allowance, it may be beneficial to claim both.

Any New Style Job Seekers Allowance you receive will be taken into account as income for Universal Credit and you may not be better off. However, by claiming and qualifying for New Style Job Seekers Allowance you’ll be awarded Class 1 National credits, which can help towards you State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

Job Seekers Allowance is a payment to support you while you’re looking for work. While receiving it, you’ll also be awarded Class 1 National credits, which can help towards you State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

It's paid every 2 weeks, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account, you can receive it up to 182 days (about 6 months).

If you want to get Universal Credit and New Style JSA at the same time, you'll need to apply for Universal Credit first.
