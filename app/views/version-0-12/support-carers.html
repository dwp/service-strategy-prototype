Financial support if you're caring for another adult

You might be able to apply for the following benefits.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

Eligibility

You may be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

If you're already getting Tax Credits

If you're already getting Tax Credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Find out more and how to apply

Please visit the Universal Credit page for full details.

What you need to apply now

You'll need:

  • your bank, building society or credit union account details (call the Universal Credit helpline if you do not have one)
  • an email address
  • information about your housing, for example how much rent you pay
  • details of your income, for example payslips
  • details of savings and any investments, like shares or a property that you rent out
  • details of how much you pay for childcare if you’re applying for help with childcare costs

If you do not provide the right information when you apply it might affect when you get paid or how much you get.

You also have to verify your identity online. You’ll need some proof of identity for this, for example your:

  • driving licence
  • passport
  • debit or credit card

Carer's Allowance

Carer's Allowance is a weekly payment to help cover the cost of caring for someone.

Eligibility

You could be eligible for Carer's allowance if:

  • you’re 16 or over
  • you spend at least 35 hours a week caring for someone
  • you normally live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • you’re not studying for 21 hours a week or more
  • you earn £128 or less a week after tax, National Insurance and expenses
  • the person you're caring for must already be getting:
    • – Personal Independence Payment - daily living component
    • – Disability Living Allowance - the middle or highest care rate
    • – Attendance Allowance
    • – Constant Attendance Allowance at or above the normal maximum rate with an Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit
    • – Constant Attendance Allowance at the basic (full day) rate with a War Disablement Pension
    • – Armed Forces Independence Payment

Carer's Credit

Carer's Credit a National Insurance credit that helps with gaps in your National Insurance record. This means you can take on caring responsibilities without affecting your ability to qualify for the State Pension.

Eligibility

You could be eligible for Carer's Credit if:

  • the person you're caring for gets certain benefits
  • you’re 16 or over
  • under State Pension age
  • you spend at least 20 hours a week caring for someone

Find out more and apply

Please visit the Carer's Credit page for full details.

Additional financial support – for the person you’re caring for

Attendance Allowance

Attendance Allowance helps the person you're caring for with extra costs. To qualify they need to be both:

  • physically or mentally disabled
  • State Pension age or older

They could also get extra Pension Credit, Housing Benefit or Council Tax Reduction if they qualify for Attendance Allowance.

They cannot get Attendance Allowance if they already get Disability Living Allowance (DLA) or Personal Independence Payment (PIP).

Find out more and how to apply

Please visit the Attendance Allowance page for full details and how to apply.

Other support

The person you are caring for might be able to get other support because of their health condition or disability. You can also browse all benefits.
