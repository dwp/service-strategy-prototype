Financial support if you're caring for a family

This page outlines the financial support you might be able to get if you're caring for a family.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

It is made up of a standard allowance and an extra amount for having children

Eligibility

You may be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

If you're already getting Tax Credits

If you're already getting Tax Credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Child benefit

You get Child Benefit if you’re responsible for bringing up a child who is:

Eligibility

You get Child Benefit if you’re responsible for bringing up a child who is:

  • under 16
  • under 20 if they stay in approved education or training

Only one person can get Child Benefit for a child, there’s no limit to how many children you can claim for.

If you choose not to get Child Benefit payments, you should still fill in and send off the claim form because by qualifying:

  • you can get National Insurance credits which count towards your State Pension
  • your child will automatically get a National Insurance number when they’re 16 years old

Can I apply for this and Universal Credit?

Yes you can, Child benefit does not impact any other benefits or claims.

Please visit the Child benefit page for full details.

Help paying for childcare

You might be able to get help with approved child care costs, including:

  • free childcare for children aged between 2 and 4
  • help with childcare costs if your child is under 16 (or under 17 and disabled)

You can get help paying for childcare if it’s provided by a:

  • registered childminder, nanny, playscheme, nursery or club
  • childminder or nanny with a registered childminder agency or childcare agency
  • registered school
  • home care worker working for a registered home care agency

Check what you can apply for

You can use the childcare calculator to check what you can apply for.

Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for children

You may be able to apply for Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for children to help with the extra costs of looking after a child who:

  • is under 16
  • has difficulties walking or needs much more looking after than a child of the same age who does not have a disability

Find out more and how to apply

Please visit the Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for children page for full details and how to apply.

Guardian’s Allowance

You could get Guardian’s Allowance if you’re bringing up a child whose parents have died. You may also be eligible if there’s one surviving parent.

The Guardian’s Allowance rate is £17.90 a week. You get it on top of Child Benefit and it’s tax-free.

Find out more and how to apply

Please visit the Guardian’s Allowance page for full details and how to apply.
