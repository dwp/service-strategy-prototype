Regular payments while you’re looking for work

The following payments are available, depending on your situation.

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

New Style JSA is a payment to support you while you’re looking for work.

You might be able to get New Style JSA at the same time as Universal Credit. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Eligibility

You might be able to get New Style JSA if you:

  • are 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 or 17 - contact Jobcentre Plus for advice)
  • are under the State Pension age
  • are not in full-time education
  • are available for work
  • are not working at the moment, or working less than 16 hours per week on average
  • cannot work but you're still being paid by your employer ('on furlough') or being paid through the Self-Employment Income Support Scheme, and usually work less than 16 hours a week
  • do not have an illness or disability which stops you from working
  • live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • have worked as an an employee and paid Class 1 National Insurance contributions, usually in the last 2 to 3 years – National Insurance credits can also count

How to apply for New Style JSA

If you get or are entitled to the severe disability premium, you might be able to get contribution based or income-based JSA.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style Jobseeker's Allowance. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

If you already get tax credits

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Eligibility

You might be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

How to apply for Universal Credit

Pension Credit

Pension Credit tops up your weekly income if it’s below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples). You may still be eligible if you have savings, a pension or you own your home.

Eligibility

You might be able to get Pension Credit if:

  • You live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • You have reached State Pension age
  • If you are in a couple, both of you will need to have reached State Pension age, or one of you is over State Pension Age and is getting Housing Benefit
  • Your weekly income is below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples)

How to apply for Pension Credit
