Regular payments if you have a health condition or disability

The following payments are available, depending on your situation.

New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

New Style ESA gives you money to help with living costs if you’re unable to work, and support to get back into work if you’re able to. You can apply for New Style ESA if you’re employed, self-employed or unemployed.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style ESA. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

Eligibility

You might be able to get New Style ESA if you:

  • are under the State Pension age
  • are disabled or have a health condition that affects how much you can work
  • or your child might have coronavirus (COVID-19) or you’re recovering from it
  • or your child are self-isolating because you came into contact with someone who might have coronavirus
  • have been told to stay at home for at least 12 weeks by the NHS because you’re at high risk of severe illness
  • have worked as an employee or been self-employed and have paid enough National Insurance contributions in the last 2 to 3 years – National Insurance Credits also count
  • do not get Statutory Sick Pay (although you can apply 3 months before it’s due to end), Jobseeker’s Allowance or the severe disability premium

How to apply for New Style ESA

Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

PIP is for adults who have difficulties with essential everyday tasks (daily living) or getting around (mobility) because of a long-term physical or mental health condition or disability.

It can help with the extra costs people face day to day to support them to lead full, active and independent lives.

You can still claim PIP if you’re working, have savings or are in training or education. PIP does not affect other benefits (except Armed Forces Independence Payment).

Getting PIP depends on your daily living or mobility needs, not the condition itself.

Eligibility

To get PIP you must:

  • be aged 16 or over and usually not have reached your State Pension age when you first claim
  • usually have lived in England, Scotland or Wales for at least 2 of the last 3 years
  • have had difficulties with daily living or mobility (or both) for 3 months because of your long-term health condition or disability, and think you’ll have difficulties for at least another 9 months

Read more information about PIP before applying

Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for children

DLA for children may help with the extra costs of looking after a child who:

  • is under 16
  • has difficulties walking or needs much more looking after than a child of the same age who does not have a disability

How to apply for DLA for children

Attendance Allowance

Attendance Allowance helps with extra costs if you have a disability severe enough that you need someone to help look after you.

You might be able to get it if:

  • you've reached State Pension age
  • you're disabled

How to apply for Attendance Allowance

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style Jobseeker's Allowance or New Style Employment and Support Allowance. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

If you already get tax credits

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Eligibility

You might be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

How to apply for Universal Credit

If you get the severe disability premium

You might be able to get the following support if you get the severe disability premium, or got it in the past month and are still eligible for it.

Income Support

Income Support can help you cover your costs if you’re on a low income.

To get it, you must:

  • be between 16 and State Pension age
  • be on a low income
  • not be working or working less than 16 hours a week, on average

You will also automatically qualify for Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction and may receive help towards health costs, such as prescriptions.

How to apply for Income Support

Working Tax Credit

If you’re in work but on low pay, you may be able to apply for Working Tax Credit to top-up your earnings.

How to apply for Working Tax Credit

Other help is also available if:

  • you're looking for work
  • you're temporarily unable to work
  • you're on a low income
  • you're caring for someone
  • you're looking after a family
  • someone has died
