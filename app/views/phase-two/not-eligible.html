You might not be able to get financial support

Based on your answers, you might not be able to get:

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

Why you might not be eligible

You can only get New Style JSA if:

  • you're under the State Pension age
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions for 16 and 17 year olds)
  • you’re available for work
  • you’re not in full time education
  • you’re not working, or are working less than 16 hours a week
  • you do not have an illness or disability which stops you from working
  • you have paid or been credited with enough National Insurance contributions over the last 2 to 3 years

How to apply for New Style JSA:

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland

Universal Credit

Why you might not be eligible

You can only get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re under State Pension age, or your partner is
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

If you want to apply anyway, find out how to apply for Universal Credit.

Pension Credit

Why you might not be eligible

You can only get Pension Credit if:

  • you have reached State Pension age
  • your partner – if you have one – has also reached State Pension age, unless you’re getting Housing Benefit
  • Your weekly income is below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples)

If you want to apply for Pension Credit anyway, find out how:

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland

Help with specific living costs

Depending on your circumstances there may be additional help you can get with specific living costs.

Other help

Other help might be available if:

  • you're disabled or have a health condition
  • you're caring for someone
  • you're looking after a family
  • someone has died
