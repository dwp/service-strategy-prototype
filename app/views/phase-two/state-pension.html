Pension Credit

Why you might be eligible

Eligibility

You might be able to get Pension Credits if:

  • You live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • You have reached State Pension age
  • If you are in a couple, both of you will need to have reached State Pension age, or one of you is over State Pension Age and is getting Housing Benefit
  • Your weekly income is below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples)

Universal Credit

Why you might be eligible

To get it, you or your partner must be under State Pension age, and you must have £16,000 or less in savings between you.

You might be able to get Universal Credit at the same time as New Style Jobseeker's Allowance. You'll need to apply for each benefit separately.

If you're already getting tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

We do not think you will be able to get New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA).

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

New Style JSA - why you might not be eligible

You can only get New Style JSA if:

  • you're under the State Pension age
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions for 16 and 17 year olds)
  • you’re available for work
  • you’re not in full time education
  • you’re not working, or are working less than 16 hours a week
  • you do not have an illness or disability which stops you from working
  • you have paid or been credited with enough National Insurance contributions over the last 2 to 3 years

How to apply for New Style JSA:

  • in England, Scotland and Wales
  • in Northern Ireland
