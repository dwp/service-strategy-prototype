Get financial support if you have low or no income

You might be able to get the following financial support if you're struggling to cover living costs. You do not have to be unemployed to get support – you could be earning a low income or retired.

Getting regular payments

If you're unemployed or on a low income:

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

Eligibility

You may be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

If you already get tax credits

If you're already getting Tax Credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Next steps

Get more information about Universal Credit.

How to apply

You'll need:

  • your bank, building society or credit union account details (call the Universal Credit helpline if you do not have one)
  • an email address
  • information about your housing, for example how much rent you pay
  • details of your income, for example payslips
  • details of savings and any investments, like shares or a property that you rent out
  • details of how much you pay for childcare if you’re applying for help with childcare costs

If you do not provide the right information when you apply it might affect when you get paid or how much you get.

You also have to verify your identity online. You’ll need some proof of identity for this, for example your:

  • driving licence
  • passport
  • debit or credit card

If you've worked in the last 2 to 3 years:

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance is a payment to support you while you’re looking for work. While receiving it, you’ll also be awarded Class 1 National Insurance credits, which can help towards your State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

It's paid every 2 weeks, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account. You can receive it for up to 182 days (about 6 months).

Eligibility

You might be able to get New Style Jobseeker's Allowance if you:

  • are 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 or 17 - contact Jobcentre Plus for advice)
  • are under the State Pension age
  • are not in full-time education
  • are available for work
  • are not working at the moment, or working less than 16 hours per week on average
  • do not have an illness or disability which stops you from working
  • live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • have the right to work in the UK
  • have worked as an an employee and paid Class 1 National Insurance contributions, usually in the last 2 to 3 years. National Insurance credits can also count.

Applying for Universal Credit at the same time

You can apply New Style Jobseeker's Allowance as well as Universal Credit.

Any New Style Jobseeker's Allowance you receive will be taken into account as income for Universal Credit and you may not be better off. By claiming New Style Jobseeker's Allowance you’ll get Class 1 National credits, which can help towards your State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

Next steps

Get more information about New Style Jobseeker's Allowance.

How to apply

You’ll need:

  • your National Insurance number
  • your bank or building society account details (or those of a family member or trusted friend)
  • your employment details for the past 6 months, including employer contact details and dates you worked with them
  • your private pension statement letter
  • to be 18 or over, if you want to apply online
Apply now

If you cannot apply online

If you need help applying or you’re aged 16 to 17, contact Jobcentre Plus.

If you've reached State Pension age:

Pension Credit

Pension Credit tops up your weekly income if it’s below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples). You may still be eligible if you have savings, a pension or you own your home.

Eligibility

You might be able to get Pension Credit if you:

  • live in England, Scotand or Wales
  • have reached State Pension age

If you’re in a couple you can start getting Pension Credit if either:

  • you and your partner have both reached State Pension age
  • one of you is getting Housing Benefit for people over State Pension age

If you qualify for Pension Credit it will mean you also qualify for other benefits, like Housing Benefit, Council Tax Reduction, Cold Weather Payment and help with the costs of NHS services.

Your State Pension

If you have not already, you might be able to start claiming your State Pension.

  • Check your state pension age
  • Get your state pension

Next steps

Get more information about Pension Credit.

How to apply

You can start your application up to 4 months before you reach State Pension age.

You can claim any time after you reach State Pension age but your claim can only be backdated for 3 months. This means you can get up to 3 months of Pension Credit in your first payment if you were eligible during that time.

You will need:

  • your National Insurance number
  • information about your income, savings and investments
  • to have already claimed your State Pension
  • to apply by phone or post if there are children included in your claim

If you’re backdating your claim, you’ll need details of your income, savings and investments on the date you want your claim to start.

Apply now

Other ways to apply

You can also apply for Pension Credit by phone or post. You'll need your bank account details.

It is taking longer than usual to apply by phone because of coronavirus (COVID-19).

A friend or family member can call for you if you cannot use the phone.

Getting help with bills and other living expenses

Budgeting Advance

You might be able to get a Budgeting Advance to help with:

  • emergency household costs such as replacing a broken cooker
  • getting a job or staying in work
  • funeral costs

To get a Budgeting Advance you must have been getting Universal Credit, Employment and Support Allowance, Income Support, Jobseeker’s Allowance or State Pension Credit for 6 months or more, unless you need the money to help you start a new job or stay in work.

Find out more and how to apply for a Budgeting Advance

Budgeting Loans

A Budgeting Loan can help with certain one-off expenses. You may be eligible for a Budgeting Loan if you’ve been on the following benefits for the past 6 months:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Pension Credit

Find out more and how to apply for Budgeting Loans

Bus Pass (Older Persons)

If you are an older person, you may be able to get a bus pass for free travel.

Find out more and how to apply for free bus travel.

Cold Weather Payment

  • Pension Credit
  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Universal Credit
  • Support for Mortgage Interest

Find out more about Cold Weather Payments

Council Tax Reduction

You could be eligible if you’re on a low income or claim benefits. Your bill could be reduced by up to 100%. You can apply if you own your home, rent, are unemployed or working.

Find out more and how to apply for free bus travel

Housing Benefit

Housing Benefit can help you pay your rent if you’re unemployed, on a low income or claiming benefits. You can make a new claim for Housing Benefit if any of the following apply:

  • you’re getting the severe disability premium, or are entitled to it
  • you got or were entitled to the severe disability premium within the last month and are still eligible for it
  • you have reached State Pension age
  • you’re in supported, sheltered or temporary housing

Find out more and how to apply for Housing Benefit

Support for Mortgage Interest loan

If you’re a homeowner, you might be able to get help towards interest payments on:

  • your mortgage
  • loans you’ve taken out for certain repairs and improvements to your home

To qualify you usually need to be getting one of the following:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Universal Credit
  • Pension Credit

Find out more and how to apply for Support for Mortgage Interest

Warm Home Discount

You could get £140 off your electricity bill for winter 2020 to 2021 under the Warm Home Discount Scheme.

You might qualify for the Warm Home Discount Scheme if:

  • you get Pension Credit
  • you’re on a low income and meet your energy supplier’s criteria for the scheme

Find out more and how to apply for Warm Home Discount Scheme

Winter Fuel Payment

If you were born on or before 5 October 1954 you could get between £100 and £300 to help you pay your heating bills.

Find out more and how to apply for Winter Fuel Payment

Other support

Citizens Advice give further advice on what to do if you can't pay bills like your mortgage, council tax or court fines.

If you have children, Citizens Advice can also help you check if they can get free school meals.

Other financial support might be available if:

  • you're caring for another adult
  • you're looking after children, or about to
  • you're disabled or have a health condition
  • someone has died
