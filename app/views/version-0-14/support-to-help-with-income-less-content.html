Getting regular payments if you have low or no income

You might be able to get the following financial support if you're struggling to cover living costs. You do not have to be unemployed to get support – you could be earning a low income or retired.

If you're unsure which benefits might be best for you, you can answer some questions to find out.

If you're unemployed or on a low income:

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

If you already get tax credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Check Universal Credit eligibility information carefully before applying.

If you've worked in the last 2 to 3 years:

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance is a payment to support you while you’re looking for work. While receiving it, you’ll also be awarded Class 1 National Insurance credits, which can help towards your State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

It's paid every 2 weeks, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account. You can receive it for up to 182 days (about 6 months).

You can apply New Style Jobseeker's Allowance as well as Universal Credit but you may not be better off, depending on your situation.

Check New Style Jobseeker's Allowance eligibility information carefully before applying.

If you've reached State Pension age:

Pension Credit

Pension Credit tops up your weekly income if it’s below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples). You may still be eligible if you have savings, a pension or you own your home.

If you have not already, you might be able to get your State Pension.

Check Pension Credit eligibility information carefully before applying.

You might be able to get help with paying your bills and living expenses.
