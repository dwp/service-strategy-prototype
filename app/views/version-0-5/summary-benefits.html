You might be able to get financial support

Based on your answers so far, you can apply for the following benefits:

  • Universal Credit
  • New Style Employment and Support Allowance
  • Carer's Allowance

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help with your living costs if you’re on a low income, out of work or cannot work.

You could get it if:

  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

Find out more about eligibility for Universal Credit.

New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

New Style ESA is a payment to help with your living costs if you if you have a disability or health condition that affects how much you can work.

You could get it if:

  • you're under State Pension age
  • you do not already get Statutory Sick Pay or Jobseeker's Allowance
  • you do not already get the severe disability premium
  • you've worked as an employee or been self-employed
  • you've paid enough National Insurance contributions, usually in the last 2 to 3 years - National Insurance credits also count

Find out more about eligibility for New Style ESA.

Apply now

Carer's Allowance

Carer's Allowance is a weekly payment to help cover the cost of caring for someone.

You could get it if:

  • the person you're caring for gets certain benefits
  • you’re 16 or over
  • you spend at least 35 hours a week caring for someone
  • you normally live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • you’re not studying for 21 hours a week or more
  • you earn £128 or less a week after tax, National Insurance and expenses

Find out more about eligibility for Carer's Allowance.
