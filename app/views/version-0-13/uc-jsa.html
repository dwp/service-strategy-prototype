Support you might be able to get

Based on your answers you might be eligible to receive both Universal Credit and New Style Job Seeker's Allowance.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to help if you are on a low income or unemployed. It is paid once a month, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account.

Eligibility

You may be able to get Universal Credit if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 to 17)
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

If you already get tax credits

If you're already getting Tax Credits, they will stop when you or your partner applies for Universal Credit.

Get more information about Universal Credit.

How to apply

You'll need:

  • your bank, building society or credit union account details (call the Universal Credit helpline if you do not have one)
  • an email address
  • information about your housing, for example how much rent you pay
  • details of your income, for example payslips
  • details of savings and any investments, like shares or a property that you rent out
  • details of how much you pay for childcare if you’re applying for help with childcare costs

If you do not provide the right information when you apply it might affect when you get paid or how much you get.

You also have to verify your identity online. You’ll need some proof of identity for this, for example your:

  • driving licence
  • passport
  • debit or credit card

New Style Job Seeker's Allowance

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance is a payment to support you while you’re looking for work. While receiving it, you’ll also be awarded Class 1 National Insurance credits, which can help towards your State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

It's paid every 2 weeks, usually into your bank, building society or credit union account. You can receive it for up to 182 days (about 6 months).

Eligibility

You might be able to get New Style Jobseeker's Allowance if you:

  • are 18 or over (there are some exceptions if you’re 16 or 17 - contact Jobcentre Plus for advice)
  • are under the State Pension age
  • are not in full-time education
  • are available for work
  • are not working at the moment, or working less than 16 hours per week on average
  • do not have an illness or disability which stops you from working
  • live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • have the right to work in the UK
  • have worked as an an employee and paid Class 1 National Insurance contributions, usually in the last 2 to 3 years. National Insurance credits can also count.

Applying for Universal Credit at the same time

You can apply New Style Jobseeker's Allowance as well as Universal Credit.

Any New Style Jobseeker's Allowance you receive will be taken into account as income for Universal Credit and you may not be better off. By claiming New Style Jobseeker's Allowance you’ll get Class 1 National credits, which can help towards your State Pension and other benefits you might need in the future.

How to apply

You’ll need:

  • your National Insurance number
  • your bank or building society account details (or those of a family member or trusted friend)
  • your employment details for the past 6 months, including employer contact details and dates you worked with them
  • your private pension statement letter
  • to be 18 or over, if you want to apply online
