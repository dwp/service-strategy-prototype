How to arrange the funeral

The funeral can usually only take place after registering the death.

There are restrictions in place during the coronavirus (COVID-19) outbreak which will affect the type of funeral that can be held. These include:

  • a limit on how many people can attend – in most cases it should only be people the person lived with or immediate family
  • social distancing measures to be in place – this means keeping 2 metres (6ft) apart from people outside your household at all times

A small number of close friends can go to the funeral if there are no household or family members who can attend.

Anyone who is unwell with coronavirus (COVID-19) symptoms should not attend. People who are self-isolating because someone in their household is ill may attend if they do not have symptoms themselves.

Funeral directors can give more help and guidance about what is possible during the coronavirus (COVID-19) outbreak.

You can find out more about arranging a funeral and finding a funeral director. You can also arrange the funeral yourself by contacting your local council.

Help with funeral costs

You could be eligible for help with funeral costs if you have a low income and get certain benefits. The amount you get will depend on your circumstances. It will not usually cover all of the costs of the funeral.

You might be able to get other help to pay for the funeral if you do not get any benefits.

Support for a child’s funeral costs

The Children’s Funeral Fund for England can help to pay for some of the costs of a funeral for a child under 18 or a baby stillborn after the 24th week of pregnancy. The burial or cremation must take place in England. It is not means-tested – what you earn or how much you have in savings will not affect what you get.

You do not have to pay for a child’s burial or cremation in Scotland or Wales.
