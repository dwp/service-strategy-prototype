Benefits and support that could help

You might be able to apply for the following benefits. Read the details we link to before applying, or answer some questions to find out what support fits your situation.

Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a payment to support living costs if you're out of work, working less or on a low income.

You could get it if:

  • you’re on a low income or out of work
  • you’re under State Pension age (or your partner is)
  • you and your partner have £16,000 or less in savings between you
  • you live in the UK

Check how to apply

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance

New Style Jobseeker's Allowance is a payment to help when you’re looking for work.

You could get it if:

  • you’re under State Pension age
  • you’ve been employed in the last 2 to 3 years and paid National Insurance contributions
  • you're not working at the moment, or you’re working less than 16 hours a week
  • you live in England, Scotland or Wales
  • you have the right to work in the UK

Check how to apply

Pension Credit

Pension Credit is a payment to help if you're over State Pension age and on a low income.

You could get it if:

  • you’re over State Pension age
  • Your weekly income is below £173.75 (for single people) or £265.20 (for couples)
  • you live in England, Scotland or Wales

Check how to apply

Budgeting Loan

A Budgeting Loan can help with one-off expenses.

You could get it if you already get:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Pension Credit

Check how to apply

Prescription prepayment certificate (PPC)

A PPC can make your prescriptions cheaper.

You could get it if you live in England.

Check how to get a PPC

Winter Fuel Payment

Winter Fuel Payment can help to cover your heating bills.

You could get it if you were born before 5 October 1954.

Check how to apply

Support for Mortgage interest

Support for Mortgage interest can help towards interest payments on your mortgage.

You could get it if you already get:

  • Universal Credit
  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Pension Credit

Check how to apply

Housing Benefit

Housing Benefit can help you pay your rent if you’re unemployed, on a low income or claiming benefits.

You could get it if:

  • you’re getting the severe disability premium, or are entitled to it
  • you got or were entitled to the severe disability premium within the last month and are still eligible for it
  • you have reached State Pension age
  • you’re in supported, sheltered or temporary housing

Check how to apply

Council Tax Reduction

Apply for a discount on your Council Tax bill if you’re on a low income or claim benefits.

Check how to apply

Other financial support

There is also financial support available if:

  • you're caring for another adult
  • you're looking after children, or about to
  • you're disabled or have a health condition
  • someone has died
